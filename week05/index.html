Ways of Seeing

Episodes • Reflection

Move your mouse (or touch) to reveal the text beneath the painting.

Episode 1 — Seeing Comes Before Words

Watching Episode 1, I realized I rush to explain images before I actually see them. When I slow down, I notice how the medium—camera moves, cropping, lighting—pushes my eyes and quietly edits my thoughts.

Episode 2 — The Female Nude and the Spectator

Episode 2 made me uncomfortable in a useful way. I caught myself looking with the trained gaze Berger describes—the split between “being” and “being looked at.” Now I pause and ask whether I’m seeing a person or a performance built for my gaze.

Episode 3 — Oil Painting & Property

Episode 3 changed how I read luxury images. Framed like oil paintings, they turn ownership into a virtue. I’ve started asking what the picture hides—labor, cost, context—when it makes property glow.

Episode 4 — Publicity & Envy

Episode 4 clicked everything together: publicity sells a future version of me, powered by envy. Naming the ask (“feel lack, buy promise”) breaks the spell.
