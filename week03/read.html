Reflection — The Medium is the Massage

By Jenny Dong

I approached McLuhan thinking the “message” was what mattered, but this reading kept nudging me to notice how the medium reshapes my attention, my habits, and even my sense of self. Reading it today, I felt how media pull me into other people’s lives instantly, and how my reactions become part of the environment.

What surprised me most was realizing that my confusion isn’t a failure; it’s a signal that I’m standing in the doorway between older habits (print, linearity) and newer ones (simultaneity, pattern recognition). That helped me read the book less like a thesis and more like a collage that trains my perception.

“All media work us over completely… The medium is the message.”

As I finished, I caught myself auditing my own tools: the way I skim, the way I scroll, the way I expect instant answers. If the medium is the message, then the message I’m practicing is speed—and I’m deciding how much of that I want to carry forward.
